# How to Draw a Perfect 5 Point Star in Photoshop

> Source: [https://www.photoshopessentials.com/basics/draw-5-point-star/](https://www.photoshopessentials.com/basics/draw-5-point-star/)
> Downloaded and converted to Markdown.

<img alt="How to draw a perfect 5 point star in Photoshop" src="https://www.photoshopessentials.com/newsite/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/draw-5-point-star-photoshop-f-250x250.png"/>

Learn how easy it is to draw a perfect 5 point star, or 5 sided star, in Photoshop! And how to save the star as a custom shape so you'll never need to draw it again. For Photoshop 2021 or later.

In this tutorial, I show you how to draw a perfect 5 point star in Photoshop. By "perfect", I mean a star where the sides of the star segments line up perfectly with each other. Now Photoshop does include a few built-in star shapes, but none of them look right. So I'll show you how easy it is to draw your own, and how to save the star as a custom shape so you'll never need to draw it again.

Here's what the star will look like when we're done. Of course, you can choose any color you like:

<img alt="How to draw a perfect 5 point star in Photoshop" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/5-point-star-photoshop.png"/>
*A perfect 5 point star drawn in Photoshop.*

Let's get started!

## Which version of Photoshop do I need?

Before we begin, I should point out that Adobe made changes to the shape tools in Photoshop 2021, which is what I'm using. So to follow along, you'll need Photoshop 2021 or newer. [Get the latest Photoshop version here](https://adobe.prf.hn/click/camref:1100lrdjJ/destination:https%3A%2F%2Fwww.adobe.com%2Fproducts%2Fphotoshop.html).

Start by creating a new document, which we'll use to draw the star. If you are on Photoshop's Home Screen, click the **Create new** button:

*Clicking "Create New" on the Home Screen.*

Or if you are in Photoshop's main interface, go up to the **File** menu in the Menu Bar and choose **New**:

<img alt="Choosing New in Photoshop's File menu" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/file-new.png"/>
*Going to File > New in the Menu Bar.*

In the New Document dialog box, choose the **Default Photoshop Size**:

<img alt="Choosing the default Pohotoshop document size in the New Document dialog box" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/default-photoshop-size.png"/>
*Choosing the default document size.*

Then click **Create**:

<img alt="Clicking the Create button in Photoshop's New Document dialog box" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/create-button.png"/>
*Clicking the Create button.*

And the new document appears:

<img alt="The new Photoshop document" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/new-photoshop-document.jpg"/>
*The new Photoshop document.*

## Step 2: Select the Polygon Tool

To draw the star, we'll use Photoshop's Polygon Tool. In the toolbar, click and hold on the **Rectangle Tool** (or whichever shape tool you used last) until a fly-out menu appears:

<img alt="Clicking and holding on the Rectangle Tool in Photoshop's toolbar" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/click-and-hold-rectangle-tool.png"/>
*Clicking and holding on the Rectangle Tool.*

And select the **Polygon Tool** from the list:

<img alt="Choosing the Polygon Tool in Photoshop's toolbar" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/select-polygon-tool.png"/>
*Choosing the Polygon Tool.*

## Step 3: Reset the tool's options

Then in the Options Bar, **right-click** (Win) / **Control-click** (Mac) on the tool's icon:

<img alt="Right-click (Win) / Control-click (Mac) on the tool icon in Photoshop's Options Bar" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/tool-icon-options-bar.png"/>
*Right-click (Win) / Control-click (Mac) on the tool icon.*

And choose **Reset Tool** from the menu:

<img alt="Resetting the Polygon Tool's options" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/reset-tool.png"/>
*Resetting the Polygon Tool's options.*

This should set the **Tool Mode** to **Shape** (not Path or Pixels):

<img alt="The Tool Mode in the Options Bar set to Shape" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/shape-tool-mode.png"/>
*The Tool Mode should be set to Shape.*

And it should set the **Number of Sides** for the polygon to **5** (since we want a 5 point star) and the **Corner Radius** to **0** (so we draw a star with sharp corners):

<img alt="The Number of Sides and Corner Radius options for the Polygon Tool" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/polygon-tool-sides-corner-radius.png"/>
*Number of sides = 5, Corner Radius = 0.*

## Step 4: Choose a fill color

You can choose a color for the shape by clicking the **Fill color swatch**:

<img alt="Clicking the Fill color swatch to choose a shape color." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/fill-color-swatch.png"/>
*Clicking the Fill color swatch.*

Then clicking the **Color Picker icon**:

<img alt="Clicking the Color Picker icon." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/color-picker-icon.png"/>
*Clicking the Color Picker icon.*

And choosing a color from the Color Picker. Any color will work. I'll choose a dark purple by setting the **Hue** (H) to **280**, the **Saturation** (S) to **75** and the **Brightness** (B) to **40.** Click OK to close the Color Picker when you're done:

<img alt="Choosing a color for the star shape." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/color-picker-choose-purple.jpg"/>
*Choosing a shape color.*

## Step 5: Turn off the stroke

By default, Photoshop adds a 1 pixel black stroke around shapes, which I don't want. So still in the Options Bar, I'll click the **Stroke color swatch**:

<img alt="Clicking the Stroke color swatch." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/click-stroke-color-swatch.png"/>
*Clicking the Stroke color swatch.*

And I'll choose the **No Color** option. Then I'll press **Enter** (Win) / **Return** (Mac) to close the dialog box:

<img alt="Choosing No Color for the stroke around the shape." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/no-color-option-stroke.png"/>
*Choosing No Color for the stroke.*

## Step 6: Draw a polygon shape

Next, we'll draw an initial polygon shape. Click in the upper left of the canvas to set a starting point for the shape, keep your mouse button held down, and begin dragging towards the bottom right:

<img alt="Clicking and dragging a polygon shape with Photoshop's Polygon Tool." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/begin-dragging-polygon-shape.png"/>
*Click and drag to begin drawing the shape.*

Once you've started dragging, press and hold the **Shift** key on your keyboard to constrain the shape so that all five sides are the same length, and continue dragging:

<img alt="Holding Shift was dragging with the Polygon Tool." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/force-all-five-sides-to-same-length.png"/>
*Holding Shift while dragging to make all five sides the same length.*

You can move and reposition the shape as you draw it by still keeping the Shift key down, holding the **spacebar** on your keyboard (so Shift+spacebar), and dragging the shape into place. I'll move it up a bit higher:

<img alt="Holding the spacebar to move the polygon shape as I draw it." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/move-shape-while-dragging.png"/>
*Add the spacebar to move the shape as you draw it.*

Then release your spacebar, but again keep the Shift key held down, and continue dragging out the rest of the shape:

<img alt="Drawing the rest of the polygon shape." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/finish-dragging-polygon-shape.png"/>
*Drawing the rest of the polygon shape.*

When you're done, release your mouse button, then release the Shift key, and Photoshop fills the shape with your chosen color:

<img alt="Releasing my mouse button to complete the polygon shape." src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/polygon-shape-drawn.png"/>
*Release your mouse button (then the Shift key) to complete the shape.*

## Step 7: Lower the Star Ratio

At this point, we have a five-sided polygon with all five sides the same length. But how do we turn it into a star?

The answer is in the **Properties panel**. Here you'll find the **Star Ratio** option, with an icon that looks like a star inside a larger polygon shape. By default, the value is set to **100 percent**:

<img alt="The Star Ratio option for the polygon shape in Photoshop's Properties panel" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/properties-panel-star-ratio.png"/>
*The Star Ratio option in the Properties panel.*

*Star Ratio* refers to the size of the star within the polygon shape. So at 100 percent, the star is taking up 100 percent of the maximum area inside the shape, which is why it doesn't look like a star. But as we lower the value (by clicking on the Star Ratio icon, keeping your mouse button held down and dragging to the left):

<img alt="Lowering the Star Ratio value" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/lower-star-ratio-80percent.png"/>
*Lowering the Star Ratio value.*

Notice that the edges of the polygon start to bend inward, and the star begins to appear (you'll need to release your mouse button for the shape to update):

<img alt="Lowering the Star Ratio option to create the star" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/star-segments-ratio-80percent.png"/>
*Lowering the ratio creates the star shape.*

To convert the polygon to a perfect star where the sides of the segments line up with each other, lower the Star Ratio to **47 percent**:

<img alt="Lowering the Star Ratio to 47 percent creates a perfect star shape in Photoshop" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/star-ratio-47percent.png"/>
*Setting the Star Ratio to 47 percent.*

And now the star segments like up perfectly:

<img alt="A perfect 5 point star created by setting the Star Ratio to 47 percent in Photoshop" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/perfect-5-point-star.png"/>
*A perfect star.*

## Step 8: Save the star as a custom shape

So that's how easy it is to draw a perfect 5 point star in Photoshop. But things get even easier at this point because now that we've drawn the star once, we can save it as a custom shape and never have to draw it again.

### Creating a new shape group

But before we do, let's create a new shape group to hold our custom shapes. Open the **Shapes panel** by going up to the **Window** menu and choosing **Shapes**:

<img alt="Opening the Shapes panel in Photoshop" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/open-shapes-panel-photoshop.png"/>
*Going to Window > Shapes.*

Then in the Shapes panel, click the **New Group** icon at the bottom:

<img alt="Creating a new shape group in Photoshop's Shapes panel" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/shapes-panel-new-group-icon.png"/>
*Creating a new shape group.*

Give the group a name, like "My Shapes", and click OK:

<img alt="Naming the new shape group" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/name-new-group.png"/>
*Naming the new group.*

The new group appears below the others. If you already created a group in the past, make sure it's selected since we want the star shape to be added to it:

<img alt="The new shape group appears in the Shapes panel" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/new-shape-group-added.png"/>
*The new shape group.*

### Creating the new custom shape

Then go up to the **Edit** menu in the Menu Bar and choose **Define Custom Shape**:

<img alt="Choosing Define Custom Shape from Photoshop's Edit menu" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/define-custom-shape.png"/>
*Going to Edit > Define Custom Shape.*

Give the shape a name. I'll name it "Perfect 5 Point Star". Then click OK:

<img alt="Naming the new custom shape" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/name-custom-star-shape.png"/>
*Naming the new custom shape.*

And back in the Shapes panel, the star appears as a thumbnail within the group:

<img alt="The star shape is added to the Shapes panel as a custom shape" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/star-saved-as-custom-shape.png"/>
*The star shape is added as a custom shape.*

### How to use the custom star shape

Since we've saved the star as a custom shape, I'll delete my original star by going to the Layers panel and dragging the shape layer down onto the trash bin:

<img alt="Deleting the original star shape in Photoshop's Layers panel" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/delete-shape-layer.png"/>
*Deleting the original star shape in the Layers panel.*

The next time you need to draw a perfect 5 point star, just open the Shapes panel (Window > Shapes) and drag and drop the star from the panel into your document:

<img alt="Dragging the custom star shape from the Shapes panel and dropping it into the Photoshop document" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/drag-and-drop-shape.png"/>
*Dragging and dropping the star shape.*

Release your mouse button and the star instantly appears. Drag the **handles** to resize the star as needed, and then press **Enter** (Win) / **Return** (Mac) to accept it:

<img alt="The star is drawn when you release your mouse button" src="https://pe-images.s3.amazonaws.com/basics/2021/shapes/draw-5-point-star/custom-star-shape-added-to-document.png"/>
*The star is drawn when you release your mouse button.*
