# Stack Floating Panels

- Entry Type: Workspace behavior
- Category: Get started > Learn the basics
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/get-started/learn-the-basics/stack-floating-panels.html

**Function Summary**
This workspace function lets you combine multiple floating panels into a single movable stack. It is used to organize the interface more efficiently, especially when you want related panels grouped together without docking them back into the main workspace columns.

**What This Function Does**
- Groups multiple floating panels into one stacked unit
- Lets the entire stack move together
- Keeps related workspace controls close to each other
- Reduces interface clutter when several panels are floating at once

**Where You Use It**
- Inside the Photoshop workspace
- While panels are floating rather than docked
- During workspace customization and layout management

**Primary Interface Elements**
- panel tab
- floating panel
- blue drop outline
- narrow drop zone between panels
- title bar of the panel stack

**How To Use It**
1. Start with at least one floating panel.
2. Drag the tab of another floating panel.
3. Move it toward the lower edge of another floating panel until the blue drop outline appears.
4. Drop the panel into the narrow stacking zone, not onto the title bar.
5. Reorder panels in the stack by dragging their tabs up or down.
6. Remove a panel from the stack by dragging its tab away from the group.

**User Interface Behavior**
- A stacked group behaves like one movable block when dragged from the top title bar.
- Dropping a panel in the wrong place may dock it instead of stacking it.
- Double-clicking a tab can minimize or expand the visible panel area depending on the workspace state.
- Panel edges can still be dragged to resize the floating group.

**Expected Result**
- Panels remain floating but are grouped together.
- The stack can be repositioned as one unit.
- Workspace organization becomes easier on smaller screens or dense layouts.

**Practical Use Cases**
- Keep `Layers`, `Properties`, and `History` close together while painting or compositing.
- Build a temporary compact control group for a specific editing task.
- Separate frequently used panels from the default dock without losing organization.
