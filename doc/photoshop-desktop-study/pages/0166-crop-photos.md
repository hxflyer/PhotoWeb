# Crop Photos

- Entry Type: Basic crop workflow
- Category: Crop, resize, and transform > Crop and straighten
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/crop-resize-transform/crop-straighten/crop-photos.html

**Function Summary**
The `Crop Tool` trims an image to improve framing, remove unwanted areas, or prepare a new composition.

**What This Function Does**
- removes outer image areas
- reframes the subject
- supports rotating the crop box before committing

**Where You Find It**
- `Crop Tool` in the toolbar

**Primary Interface Elements**
- crop border
- corner and edge handles
- interior drag area
- `Done`
- contextual task bar

**How To Use It**
1. Select the `Crop Tool`.
2. Drag the crop handles to define the area you want to keep.
3. Drag inside the crop area if you want to reposition the frame.
4. Hover just outside a corner and drag to rotate the crop when needed.
5. Commit with `Enter`, `Return`, or `Done`.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- Mouse: drag handles to resize, drag inside the box to reposition, drag outside a corner to rotate.
- Keyboard: `Enter` on Windows or `Return` on macOS to commit.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop darkens the area outside the crop box to show what will be removed.
- Rotation is handled directly on-canvas with the same crop frame.

**Expected Result**
- The image is trimmed to the chosen composition and optionally straightened through crop rotation.
