# Remove Background in Your Images

- Entry Type: Quick Action workflow
- Category: Repair and retouch > Remove objects and fill space
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/repair-retouch/remove-objects-fill-space/remove-background-in-your-images.html

**Function Summary**
`Remove Background` automatically isolates a subject and hides or removes the background through a quick action.

**What This Function Does**
- detects the main subject
- creates a transparent or masked background result
- provides a fast starting point for product images, portraits, and composites

**Where You Find It**
- `Discover > Browse > Quick Actions > Remove Background`
- `Help > Photoshop Help` to open Discover

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Discover` panel
- `Quick Actions`
- `Remove Background`
- `Refresh`
- `Revert`
- `Layers` panel
- layer mask

**How To Use It**
1. Select and unlock the image layer if needed.
2. Open the `Discover` panel.
3. Choose `Browse > Quick Actions > Remove Background`.
4. Use `Refresh` to rerun the action on a different selected layer.
5. Use `Revert` if the result should be undone from the panel.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- Menu path: `Help > Photoshop Help`
- Mouse: click `Remove Background` in the `Quick Actions` area.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop creates an editable isolation result, usually through layer transparency or a mask.

**Expected Result**
- The subject remains visible while the background is removed or hidden.
