# Get Started with Artboards

- Entry Type: Artboard concept reference
- Category: Create and manage layers > Layout and design tools
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/create-manage-layers/layout-design-tools/get-started-artboards.html

**Function Summary**
This entry explains what artboards are, how they are structured, and why they are useful for multi-screen or multi-layout design inside one Photoshop document.

**What This Entry Explains**
- what an artboard is
- how artboards organize content
- why designers use them for multi-version layouts

**What Artboards Do**
- Create separate clipped design areas within one document
- Group content by screen, page, or variation
- Help compare multiple layouts side by side

**Key Behaviors**
- Artboards clip contained content to their boundaries
- Artboards can contain layers and layer groups
- Layers outside artboards remain separate and unclipped

**Customization Area**
- `Edit > Preferences > Interface > Appearance > Artboards`

**Expected Result**
- Users understand artboards as multi-canvas containers for organized design work in one PSD.
