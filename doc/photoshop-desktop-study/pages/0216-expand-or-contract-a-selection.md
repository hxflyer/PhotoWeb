# Expand or Contract a Selection

- Entry Type: Selection-size modification workflow
- Category: Make selections > Refine and modify selections
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/make-selections/refine-modify-selections/expand-or-contract-selection.html

**Function Summary**
These commands increase or reduce the boundary of a selection by a specified number of pixels, or grow it by similar color.

**What This Function Does**
- expands a selection outward
- contracts a selection inward
- grows selection by adjacent similar color
- selects similar color across the image

**Where You Find It**
- `Select > Modify > Expand`
- `Select > Modify > Contract`
- `Select > Grow`
- `Select > Similar`

**How To Use It**
1. Make a selection.
2. Use `Expand` or `Contract` for exact pixel-based changes.
3. Use `Grow` when you want adjacent pixels within the Magic Wand tolerance range.
4. Use `Similar` when you want matching colors throughout the document.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Pixel-based modification respects the existing border shape.
- `Grow` and `Similar` rely on color relationships instead of fixed geometry.

**Expected Result**
- The selection becomes larger, smaller, or more color-inclusive depending on the chosen command.
