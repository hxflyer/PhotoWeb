# Dock or Undock Panels

- Entry Type: Workspace layout control
- Category: Get started > Learn the basics
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/get-started/learn-the-basics/dock-undock-panels.html

**Function Summary**
This function changes whether panels are attached to a dock or floating independently. Docking keeps panels grouped in a structured vertical layout, while undocking makes them movable so the workspace can be arranged more freely.

**What This Function Does**
- Attaches panels to an existing dock
- Removes panels or groups from a dock so they float freely
- Helps build a more organized or more flexible panel layout
- Supports both single-panel and grouped-panel movement

**Where You Find It**
- Panel tabs
- panel group title bars
- `Window` menu for reopening panels that are not visible

**Primary Interface Elements**
- panel tab
- panel group
- dock
- title bar
- blue highlight drop target
- `Window` menu

**How To Use It**
1. Open the panel you want from the `Window` menu if it is not already visible.
2. To dock a panel, drag its tab toward the edge of another panel group or existing dock.
3. Watch for the blue highlight that shows a valid dock target.
4. Release the mouse when the highlight appears to place the panel in the dock.
5. To dock an entire panel group, drag it by its title bar instead of an individual tab.
6. To undock a panel or group, drag it away from the dock by its tab or title bar until it becomes free-floating.
7. Resize the dock vertically by dragging its bottom edge upward if you do not want the dock to fill the full workspace height.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- `Mouse`
  Drag tabs or title bars to dock and undock panels.
- `Menus`
  Use `Window` to open panels such as `Brushes`, `Layers`, or `Swatches` before positioning them.
- `Keyboard`
  No dedicated default hotkey is described for docking or undocking.

**User Interface Behavior**
- A dock is a vertical collection of attached panels or panel groups.
- Photoshop shows a blue highlight when the panel can be docked at the current position.
- Dragging a panel away from all dock targets makes it float.
- Docking changes workspace layout only and does not change panel content or document data.

**Expected Result**
- Panels can be organized into stable side columns or broken out into floating groups depending on your editing style.

**Practical Use Cases**
- Build a tight right-side dock for frequently used adjustment panels.
- Float color or brush panels closer to the canvas during painting.
- Separate a specialized panel group for a temporary workflow.
