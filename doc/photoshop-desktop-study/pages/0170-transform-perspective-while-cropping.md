# Transform Perspective While Cropping

- Entry Type: Perspective Crop workflow
- Category: Crop, resize, and transform > Crop and straighten
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/crop-resize-transform/crop-straighten/transform-perspective-while-cropping.html

**Function Summary**
The `Perspective Crop Tool` crops an image while correcting converging or distorted perspective lines.

**What This Function Does**
- straightens photographed rectangles such as signs, pages, or building faces
- combines crop and perspective correction in one step
- removes skewed camera-angle distortion

**Where You Find It**
- `Perspective Crop Tool` nested under the crop tool group

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Perspective Crop Tool`
- corner handles
- crop marquee
- `Enter` or `Return`

**How To Use It**
1. Open the crop tool group and choose `Perspective Crop Tool`.
2. Draw a marquee over the object you want to correct.
3. Drag the corner points so they align with the object's true edges.
4. Commit with `Enter` or `Return`.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- Mouse: drag the marquee and its corner points.
- Keyboard: `Enter` on Windows or `Return` on macOS to apply.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop maps the skewed shape to a rectangular result when the crop is committed.
- The final image is both cropped and perspective-corrected.

**Expected Result**
- A distorted object appears more front-facing and evenly aligned in the finished crop.
