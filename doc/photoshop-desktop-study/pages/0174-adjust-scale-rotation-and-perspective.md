# Adjust Scale, Rotation, and Perspective

- Entry Type: Interactive transform workflow
- Category: Crop, resize, and transform > Transform, manipulate, and reshape
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/crop-resize-transform/transform-manipulate-reshape/adjust-scale-rotation-and-perspective.html

**Function Summary**
This workflow applies interactive transform modes to resize, rotate, skew, distort, add perspective, or warp the active content.

**What This Function Does**
- changes size, angle, and shape
- supports creative distortion and correction work
- applies to layers and selected pixel regions

**Where You Find It**
- `Edit > Transform`

**Primary Interface Elements**
- transform bounding box
- corner and side handles
- `Scale`
- `Rotate`
- `Skew`
- `Distort`
- `Perspective`
- `Warp`
- `Done`

**How To Use It**
1. Select the target layer or selection.
2. Open `Edit > Transform`.
3. Choose the transform mode you need.
4. Drag handles or control points on the canvas to adjust the content.
5. Commit with `Enter`, `Return`, or `Done`.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- Mouse: drag handles to adjust the transform interactively.
- Keyboard: `Enter` on Windows or `Return` on macOS to commit.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop shows the selected content inside a transform boundary.
- Different modes expose different handle behavior, such as free corners, perspective shifts, or warp deformation.

**Expected Result**
- The active content is reshaped and committed in its new orientation or perspective.
