# Adjust Content-Aware Fill Settings

- Entry Type: Content-Aware Fill settings reference
- Category: Repair and retouch > Remove objects and fill space
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/repair-retouch/remove-objects-fill-space/adjust-content-aware-fill-settings.html

**Function Summary**
This reference explains the controls that determine how `Content-Aware Fill` samples source pixels, adapts the fill, and outputs the result.

**What This Entry Is For**
- controls the visible sampling overlay
- changes whether Photoshop samples automatically or from custom areas
- adjusts color, rotation, scale, and mirror behavior
- chooses whether results affect the current layer, a new layer, or a duplicate layer

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Show Sampling Area`
- overlay `Opacity`
- overlay `Color`
- `Auto`
- `Rectangular`
- `Custom`
- `Sampling Brush`
- `Sample All Layers`
- `Color Adaptation`
- `Rotation Adaptation`
- `Scale`
- `Mirror`
- `Output To`

**How To Use This Information**
1. Choose the sampling area mode.
2. Tune fill adaptation settings for texture, color, rotation, or scale needs.
3. Choose an output mode that preserves edit flexibility when the fill needs later review.

**Expected Result**
- Content-Aware Fill produces a more controlled fill and sends the result to the desired layer destination.
