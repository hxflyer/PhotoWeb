# Arrange and Group Panels

- Entry Type: Workspace layout control
- Category: Get started > Learn the basics
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/get-started/learn-the-basics/manipulate-panel-groups.html

**Function Summary**
This function organizes Photoshop panels into grouped tab sets so related controls stay together. It is used to create cleaner work areas and more task-focused workspace layouts.

**What This Function Does**
- Groups panels into shared tab sets
- Reorders tabs within a group
- Removes a panel from a group so it floats
- Moves an entire group as one unit

**Where You Use It**
- In the docked or floating panel area of the Photoshop workspace
- After opening the needed panels from the `Window` menu

**Primary Interface Elements**
- panel tab
- panel group
- highlighted drop zone
- floating panel
- group title bar
- `Window` menu

**How To Use It**
1. Open the panels you need from the `Window` menu.
2. Drag a panel tab into the highlighted drop zone of another panel group to combine them.
3. Drag tabs left or right inside the group to change their order.
4. Drag a tab out of the group to make that panel float independently.
5. Drag the group by its title bar when you want to move the whole set together.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- `Mouse`
  Drag panel tabs and title bars to build or reorganize panel groups.
- `Menus`
  Use `Window` to show the panels before grouping them.
- `Keyboard`
  No dedicated default hotkey is described for grouping panels.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop highlights valid group targets during the drag.
- Grouped panels share one container and are switched by clicking tabs.
- Removing a panel from the group turns it into a floating panel unless it is dropped into another dock target.
- Moving a group by the title bar keeps the whole tab set together.

**Expected Result**
- Related controls stay together in a more predictable layout.
- Workspace organization improves, especially when many panels are open at once.

**Practical Use Cases**
- Group `Layers`, `Channels`, and `Paths` together.
- Build a painting group that keeps brush-related panels in one place.
- Create a compact design workspace for smaller monitors.
