# Adjust Contrast with Clarity and Dehaze

- Entry Type: Contrast and haze correction workflow
- Category: Create and manage layers > Color adjustment and fill layers
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/create-manage-layers/color-adjustment-fill-layers/adjust-contrast-with-clarity-and-dehaze.html

**Function Summary**
This adjustment layer increases perceived detail and reduces atmospheric haze using `Clarity` and `Dehaze`. It is meant for non-destructive contrast shaping, especially in flat or hazy images.

**What This Function Does**
- Increases midtone definition with `Clarity`
- Reduces haze and boosts image separation with `Dehaze`
- Keeps the correction editable on its own adjustment layer

**Where You Find It**
- Layers panel
- `Create new fill or adjustment layer`
- `Clarity and dehaze`
- `Properties` panel

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Clarity`
- `Dehaze`
- `Properties`
- layer name field

**How To Use It**
1. Open the image you want to adjust.
2. In the Layers panel, create a new `Clarity and dehaze` adjustment layer.
3. In the `Properties` panel, increase or reduce `Clarity` to shape midtone contrast.
4. Adjust `Dehaze` to reduce haze and improve scene separation.
5. Name the layer if needed and confirm with `Enter` or `Return`.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- `Mouse`
  Create the adjustment and drag the `Clarity` and `Dehaze` sliders in the Properties panel.
- `Menus`
  This correction is launched from the layer-creation control in the Layers panel.
- `Keyboard`
  Use `Enter` or `Return` to finalize layer naming.

**User Interface Behavior**
- The correction stays non-destructive and editable while the adjustment layer remains separate.
- `Clarity` mainly affects local midtone structure, while `Dehaze` targets hazy low-contrast atmospheric conditions.

**Expected Result**
- The image appears clearer, more defined, and less washed out without permanently altering the original pixels.
