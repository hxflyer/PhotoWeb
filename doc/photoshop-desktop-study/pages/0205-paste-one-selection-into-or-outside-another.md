# Paste One Selection Into or Outside Another

- Entry Type: Paste Special masking workflow
- Category: Make selections > Refine and modify selections
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/make-selections/refine-modify-selections/paste-one-selection-into-or-outside-another.html

**Function Summary**
`Paste Into` and `Paste Outside` place copied content inside or outside a destination selection by automatically building a mask.

**What This Function Does**
- pastes copied content through a selection-defined mask
- supports in-mask repositioning after paste
- combines pasted content and mask editing in one workflow

**Where You Find It**
- `Edit > Paste Special > Paste Into`
- `Edit > Paste Special > Paste Outside`

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Paste Into`
- `Paste Outside`
- layer mask thumbnail
- `Move Tool`
- `Merge Down`

**How To Use It**
1. Copy or cut the source content.
2. Create the destination selection in the same or another document.
3. Choose `Paste Into` or `Paste Outside` from `Paste Special`.
4. Use the `Move Tool` to reposition the pasted content behind the mask.
5. Edit the layer mask if necessary.
6. Merge down later if you want to collapse the result.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- Menu path: `Edit > Paste Special > Paste Into`
- Menu path: `Edit > Paste Special > Paste Outside`
- Mouse: drag with the `Move Tool` to reposition the pasted content.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop creates a new layer plus a linked mask based on the destination selection.

**Expected Result**
- The pasted content is revealed only inside or outside the chosen selection boundary.
