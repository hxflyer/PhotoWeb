# Replace Background with Generate Background

- Entry Type: Generative background workflow
- Category: Repair and retouch > Remove objects and fill space
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/repair-retouch/remove-objects-fill-space/replace-background-with-generate-background.html

**Function Summary**
`Generate Background` replaces the area behind a subject with AI-generated, imported, or color-filled background content.

**What This Function Does**
- removes or isolates the original background
- generates new background variations from text prompts
- can use an imported image or solid color as the replacement background
- attempts to match lighting, perspective, and subject context

**Where You Find It**
- contextual task bar after opening or selecting an image
- `Window > Contextual Task Bar`

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Remove Background`
- `Generate Background`
- prompt field
- color fill icon
- `Import Background`
- `Generate`
- `Properties` panel variations

**How To Use It**
1. Open the image.
2. Use `Remove Background` from the contextual task bar.
3. Choose `Generate Background`.
4. Enter a descriptive prompt, import a background image, or choose a color fill.
5. Click `Generate`.
6. Pick a variation in the `Properties` panel or revise the prompt and generate again.

**Expected Result**
- The subject is composited against a new generated or selected background.
