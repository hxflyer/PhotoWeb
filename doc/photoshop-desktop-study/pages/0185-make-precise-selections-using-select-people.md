# Make Precise Selections Using Select People

- Entry Type: People-part selection workflow
- Category: Make selections > Automatic and color-based selections
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/make-selections/automatic-color-based-selections/make-precise-selections-using-select-people.html

**Function Summary**
`Select People` identifies people in an image and lets you target an entire person or specific parts such as hair, skin, or facial regions.

**What This Function Does**
- detects individual people in a photo
- breaks a person into selectable subregions
- speeds up portrait masking and retouch targeting

**Where You Find It**
- `Object Selection Tool` options bar

**Primary Interface Elements**
- `Select People`
- person thumbnails
- `Entire Person`
- `Hair`
- `Eyebrows`
- `Facial Skin`
- `Beard`

**How To Use It**
1. Select the `Object Selection Tool`.
2. Choose `Select People`.
3. Pick the person thumbnail you want to target.
4. Choose `Entire Person` or a specific body or face-related tag.
5. Refine manually if needed.

**Mouse, Keyboard, and Menu Input**
- Mouse: click a person thumbnail, then click one or more part tags.

**User Interface Behavior**
- Photoshop lists detected people separately and exposes targeted region chips or tags for each person.

**Expected Result**
- A person or a precise part of that person becomes selected without manual tracing.
