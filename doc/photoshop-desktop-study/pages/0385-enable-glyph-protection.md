# Enable Glyph Protection

- Entry Type: Glyph substitution protection workflow
- Category: Text and typography > Characters and glyphs
- Source: https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop/desktop/text-typography/characters-glyphs/enable-glyph-protection.html

**Function Summary**
Glyph protection helps prevent valid characters from being lost or replaced by unsupported boxes when changing fonts.

**What This Function Does**
- Warns or prevents font changes that cannot represent selected characters.
- Reduces accidental replacement of special glyphs, international characters, emoji, or symbols.
- Helps preserve text readability during font experimentation.

**Where You Find It**
- Type preferences or text/font protection settings where available.
- Font change workflows in the options bar, `Properties`, or `Character` panel.

**Primary Interface Elements**
- Font family menu.
- Character selection.
- Missing glyph warning or fallback behavior.

**How To Use It**
1. Enable glyph protection in the available type/font preference area.
2. Select text that contains special characters or non-Latin scripts.
3. Change fonts carefully and watch for warnings or unsupported glyph indicators.
4. Choose a font with proper glyph coverage if Photoshop blocks or flags the change.

**Expected Result**
- Photoshop preserves valid glyph representation more reliably during font changes.
